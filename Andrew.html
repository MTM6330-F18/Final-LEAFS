Andrew Balfour

balfoura@algonquincollege.com

Andrew Balfour is an Ottawa based portrait photographer. He specializes in photography for business, government and the arts.

Basic Question:

1. What made you decide to teach at Algonquin College?

Didn’t apply, didn’t want to be a teacher. One day the dean ask if I wanted to be a teacher. What made him a teacher is someone believing. Why am I still here. Meet people be intelligent. Had teacher that weren’t caring.

2. What are the courses did you teach in Algonquin College?

Digital Graphic, Ux design, mobile development, motion graphic, environmental guardianship.

3. Can you give me more details about the courses you’re presently teaching?

You need to learn photoshop and illustrator, the class is 4 hours a week and 4 hours your own time, assment every week, more technical, how to use the tools.

4. How hard is this program for the people who have never studied this area?

It should be hard, if it’s too easy you are not putting too much effort. Doesn’t mean hard doesn’t mean stressful.

5. How long have you been teaching in the IMD program? So far, do you think that you made an impact towards your students?

9 years, yea they told me so. I think I am getting better and better.

6. How do you measure student performance in your classroom?

Attitude, performance.

7. As a professor, what do you expect from the students?

Intelligents, hope they learn.

It’s not unique, community college, many international students, finally using Algonquin culture, not a big competitor.
